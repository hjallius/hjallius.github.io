01. A Procession of Fifty Horses

A friend told me the other day that on Bennett’s Hill, between the Wellington public house and the offices of a landscape architect, is a thin and deep-shadowed alleyway.

There, he said, one thousand rats are buried under the pavement, all murdered using a Spear & Jackson Square Head Neverbend Digging Spade.

That I am inclined to believe this tale is a testament to the prowess of the Spear & Jackson Square Head Neverbend Digging Spade which intelligent readers know to have a solid forged carbon steel head, with an extra long socket for strength, and a hammer finish epoxy coating for improved resistance to rust, scratches, humidity and alkalines in the soil.

All of which makes it the perfect instrument of justice for a drumhead court-martial of rodents. Twenty-five thousand, four hundred and fifty-seven reviews on ScrewFix just can’t be wrong.
